ABSTRACT 
 
This proposal outlines the development of Taranga, an AI-powered web-based system 
designed to screen for multiple learning disabilities and suggest personalized, appropriate 
interventions for Nepali students. Learning difficulties such as dyslexia, dysgraphia, 
dyscalculia, auditory processing disorder, and  nonverbal learning disorder often go 
undetected in Nepal due to stigma, lack of awareness, and insufficient access to clinical 
professionals. Taranga aims to address this gap through an adaptive questionnaire 
system, multi-label machine learning models, and an NLP-based observation analyzer. 
 
The system will recommend targeted strategies and include interactive activities that track 
student progress over time. To be built using FastAPI, ReactJS, and SQLAlchemy, the 
project follows a Hybrid Iterative -Waterfall methodology enhanced with prototyping , 
allowing structured documentation with flexible feature refinement. The system is 
explicitly non -diagnostic and designed for educational awareness and support. This 
proposal details the project’s objectives, technical design, methodology, risk 
management, resource planning, and expected outcomes. \n\n--- PAGE 4 ---\n

1. Introduction  
 
Millions of children around the world suffer from learning disabilities and most cases go 
undiagnosed in developing countries. In Nepal, awareness is extremely low, and students 
with learning difficulties are often  neglected. Due to the absence of early screening 
mechanisms, it is tough for teachers and parents to identify the actual cause behind a 
child’s learning problem. Lack of early detection can result in long -term effects such as 
poor academic performance, lower self-esteem, dropout rates, and emotional problems. 
While educational technology is on the rise across the world, Nepal lags in the required 
digital tools to help schools identify early signs of difficulties in learning and provide 
intervention strategies correspondingly. Taranga seeks to fill this gap with the provision 
of an AI -driven early screening  and personalized recommendation for intervention in 
children with symptoms of learning difficulties. 
 
1.1 Problem Scenario 
Learning disabilities affect a significant portion of learners worldwide, with developmental 
learning disorders impacting 5 to 15% of school -aged children  (American Psychiatric 
Association, n.d.) . Dyslexia, one of the most common learning difficulties, impacts a 
substantial portion of the population, with studies suggesting that 15 to 20% of individuals 
may show symptoms of dyslexia (International Dyslexia Association , n.d.). In developing 
countries, however, these conditions often remain undetected due to stigma and limited 
awareness. 
In Nepal, similar challenges are evident. A study conducted in Madi Municipality, Chitwan, 
found that the majority of primary school teachers had limited knowledge and no formal 
training related to learning disabilities. Only 9.5% demonstrated adequate knowledge, 
while most had moderate or inadequate understanding (Thapa, et al., 2024). As a result, 
students struggling with reading, writing, or attention are frequently mislabelled as “slow” 
or “undisciplined” rather than being screened for LDs. Human Rights Watch (2018) further 
reports a severe shortage of trained teachers, and UNICEF Nepal (2021) notes that the \n\n--- PAGE 9 ---\nFINAL YEAR PROJECT                                 CS6P05NI

country lacks standardized screening tools and structured early -intervention processes. 
These findings reveal a clear gap in early detection and structured support. 
 
1.2 Project as a Solution 
Taranga proposes an AI -powered screening and recommendation system designed 
specifically for the Nepali educational context. The system provides: 
• Adaptive questionnaires that adjust based on responses, enabling more accurate 
screening. 
• Multi-label classification to identify symptoms across several learning disabilities 
simultaneously. 
• Teacher Observation Analyzer using NLP to extract patterns and indicators of LD 
from teacher notes. 
• Personalized intervention recommendations  suggesting classroom strategies, 
home-based remedies, and learning supports. 
• AI explainability feature that help teachers and parents understand why the system 
flagged certain indicators, promoting transparency and trust in AI-driven decisions.

5 Methodology 
 
This project follows a Hybrid Iterative–Waterfall methodology enhanced with Prototyping, 
providing a balance between structured SDLC phases and the flexibility required for AI 
model development and user interface refinement. As the project is undertaken 
individually and within a fixed timeline, a full Agile approach is unsuitable. However, th e 
complexity of machine learning, NLP, and adaptive UI components requires iterative 
cycles for experimentation and improvement. The hybrid model ensures clear planning 
while enabling controlled iteration. 
 
5.1 Overview of the SDLC Approach 
5.1.1 Requirements Analysis 
This phase involves identifying and documenting both functional and non -functional 
requirements for Taranga. It includes researching learning disabilities (based on DSM -5 
and psychological literature), evaluating existing screening tools, and identifying g aps in 
the Nepali education context. Role -specific requirements (for teachers, parents, and 
admins), accessibility, data privacy, ML interpretability, and intervention mechanisms are 
all covered in this phase. 
 
5.1.2 System & Model Design (with Prototyping) 
Early-stage prototyping will be used to validate critical design assumptions. Low -fidelity 
wireframes for the dashboard, adaptive questionnaire flow, and progress tracker will be 
created and iteratively improved. At the same time, the technical design includes: 
• Database schema (ER diagrams) 
• API structure and request flows 
• ML model structure 
• NLP data extraction logic 
• User interface layouts (based on user roles) \n\n--- PAGE 17 ---\nFINAL YEAR PROJECT                                 CS6P05NI 
23048503 Sanskriti Agrahari    10 
5.1.3 Implementation (Iterative Cycles) 
The core system will be developed in five short iterative cycles , each concluding with 
internal testing and feedback review. 
• Iteration 1 : Adaptive questionnaire engine (dynamic flow logic, role -specific 
question filters, and response storage) 
• Iteration 2: Multi-label machine learning model (initially rule -based, then trained 
on mock data for LD prediction) 
• Iteration 3: NLP-based teacher observation analyzer (keyword extraction, basic 
entity recognition, free-text analysis) 
• Iteration 4 : Intervention recommendation engine and activity tracker (LD -wise 
activities, scoring metrics, and activity log storage) 
• Iteration 5: Role-based access control, dashboard visualization, and full progress 
tracker integration 
 
5.1.4 Integration & Testing (Final System Validation) 
Once all modules are individually developed, they will be fully integrated and tested in a 
local environment. Testing will include: 
• Unit testing of Python and React components 
• Validation of adaptive logic and question flows 
• API testing with FastAPI 
• Evaluation of ML/NLP modules using accuracy, recall, and explainability checks 
• Usability validation through simulated user interactions (teacher/parent use cases) 
• Cross-checking data consistency and logic integrity 
 
5.1.5 Evaluation & Documentation 
The final system will be evaluated based on: 
• AI model performance (precision, recall, multi-label accuracy) 
• NLP analyzer reliability \n\n--- PAGE 18 ---\nFINAL YEAR PROJECT                                 CS6P05NI

• Progress tracking reliability

10 Conclusion 
 
Taranga is envisioned as a socially responsible, AI -assisted screening and support 
platform for early identification of learning disabilities among school -aged children in 
Nepal. With its adaptive, data-driven approach and integration of local context, the system 
strives to empower teachers and parents with awareness and actionable strategies rather 
than clinical diagnoses. 
By following a hybrid development methodology and integrating interactive activities with 
measurable progress tracking, the project aligns both technically and ethically with its 
purpose. Through careful implementation and testing, Taranga will deliver a m eaningful, 
accessible tool that contributes toward inclusive education and early support systems in 
under-resourced settings. 
This proposal establishes the foundation for successful implementation of the project by 
detailing the goals, design plans, development strategy, and risk preparedness for the 
months ahead.
